nettles

the bed is empty. my sadness sleeps. curled up, gathered, and small, wrapped in black. the mirrors are dry. the airy reality no longer hurts. the eruption long extinguished. unrest shapes beneath the skin. a step further into quicksand. i catch irreversible time mid-flight. the film rewinds and skips, gasps, pushes, and rattles. my word is still far away. oh, dark eye, disobedient eye, shameless eye – isn’t it easier that way? while the film rewinds and skips, gasps, pushes, and rattles, i search again for my urges. i rush again into memories. eyes wet, throat in nettles.

using written material from my phone notes, messages i wrote when i wanted to tell someone something but lacked the courage/heart, i wanted to present silence as words that are there, exist, and we feel them, but remain unfinished and pale. hiding the content of texts behind my photos taken in a hurry, like snapshots, i emphasize the speed of moments when i needed to talk to someone. the fading text reflected on the other side represents that shyness, involving the viewer in the game of discovering barely visible words.

i finish the booklet with my poem “nettle,” born precisely as a result of suppressed emotions and the unspoken.
